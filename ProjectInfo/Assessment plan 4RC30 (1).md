Assessment plan of course 4RC30

Group assignment is to solve a self-defined flow problem in groups of 2 or 3 persons and then write out
a paper.

•  Students form the group on their own.
•  Students  will  define  the  problem  based  on  their  own  interest,  which  involves  at  least  two

components of

o  heat transfer
o  chemical reaction
o
turbulence

•  The flow problem needs to be approved by the teacher (Niels or Yali).
•  To solve the problem, student can either use the Matlab code supplied in the course or write their

own code. Any commercial software or open-source packages are not allowed.

•  The paper should be written in the format of the supplied template and submitted before the

given deadline.

•  After  paper  submission,  complete  the  Group  Member  Evaluation,  which  will  be  the  inputs  for

individual assessment.

Assessment
The final grade is based on a group grade (80%) and an individual grade (20%):

•  The  group  grade  is  based  on  the  assessment  of  the  report/paper  by  one  of  the  teachers.  The
assessment  utilizes  a  detailed  rubric  which  covers  components  of  problem  description  and
analysis (15%), CFD model definition and execution (45%), results and analysis (25%), and writing
(15%).

•  The  individual  grade  is  based  on  the  assessment  (by  the  teacher)  on  students’  individual
contribution (CRediT authorship at end of the report/paper) and the peer evaluation from other
group members. The group member evaluation is done using FeedbackFruits: Students will reflect
on  the  collaborating  and  contributing  process  within  groups  as  well  as  individual  CFD  skills
developed within this course (with a rubric defined by the teacher).

•  A minimum of 6 for the final grade (with a conditional grade >=5.5 for individual assessment) is

required to pass the course.

Retake opportunities are:

•  To refine and improve the paper based on the feedback from the lecturers.
•  To redo the entire final assignment.
